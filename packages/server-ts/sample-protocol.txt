PROTOCOL TITLE: A Randomized, Open-Label Phase II Study of Toripalimab Combined
with Anlotinib Versus Docetaxel in Advanced Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer
After Failure of Platinum-Based Chemotherapy

STUDY ID: NSCLC-2024-TA-001
PROTOCOL VERSION: 3.0
DATE: 2026-07-18
PRINCIPAL INVESTIGATOR: Dr. Zhang Wei
SPONSOR: Heurion Clinical Research Network
SITES: Multi-center (5 sites)

══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
1. BACKGROUND AND RATIONALE

Non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) accounts for approximately 85% of all
lung cancers. Despite advances in first-line immunotherapy, patients who
progress after platinum-based chemotherapy have limited treatment options
with median overall survival of 8-12 months.

Toripalimab (JS001) is a humanized anti-PD-1 monoclonal antibody approved
in China for melanoma and nasopharyngeal carcinoma. Anlotinib is a novel
multi-target tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI) targeting VEGFR, FGFR, PDGFR,
and c-Kit. The combination of anti-PD-1 therapy with anti-angiogenic agents
has shown synergistic anti-tumor activity in multiple tumor types.

Preclinical data suggest that anlotinib-mediated vascular normalization may
enhance T-cell infiltration, potentially augmenting the efficacy of PD-1
blockade. A Phase Ib study (N=28) demonstrated an ORR of 39.3% and
manageable safety profile for the toripalimab-anlotinib combination in
pre-treated NSCLC patients.

2. STUDY OBJECTIVES

2.1 Primary Objective
- To compare progression-free survival (PFS) between Arm A (toripalimab +
  anlotinib) and Arm B (docetaxel) in patients with advanced NSCLC who
  have progressed after platinum-based chemotherapy.

2.2 Secondary Objectives
- To compare overall survival (OS) at 24 months
- To compare objective response rate (ORR) per RECIST v1.1
- To compare disease control rate (DCR) at 12 weeks
- To evaluate safety and tolerability (CTCAE v5.0)
- To assess quality of life using EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13
- To explore biomarker correlates (PD-L1 TPS, TMB, blood-based TMB)

2.3 Exploratory Objectives
- To evaluate circulating tumor DNA (ctDNA) dynamics as early predictor
  of response
- To assess tumor immune microenvironment changes in paired biopsies
- To correlate anlotinib exposure levels with efficacy and toxicity

3. STUDY DESIGN

This is a randomized, open-label, active-controlled, parallel-group,
multi-center Phase II study.

Patients will be randomized in a 1:1 ratio to:
  Arm A (Experimental): Toripalimab 240 mg IV Q3W + Anlotinib 12 mg PO
                         daily (2 weeks on/1 week off)
  Arm B (Control):       Docetaxel 75 mg/m² IV Q3W

Stratification factors:
  - PD-L1 TPS status (< 1% vs 1-49% vs ≥ 50%)
  - Histology (squamous vs non-squamous)
  - Number of prior treatment lines (1 vs 2)

Treatment continues until disease progression, unacceptable toxicity,
patient withdrawal, or completion of 24 months of therapy.

Crossover is NOT permitted.

4. INCLUSION CRITERIA (Must meet ALL)

IC-01: Histologically or cytologically confirmed stage IIIB, IIIC, or
       IV NSCLC (AJCC 8th edition)
IC-02: Age ≥ 18 years and ≤ 75 years
IC-03: ECOG performance status 0 or 1
IC-04: At least one measurable lesion per RECIST v1.1 (target lesion
       ≥ 10 mm in longest diameter on CT scan, or ≥ 15 mm for lymph
       node short axis)
IC-05: Disease progression during or after platinum-based doublet
       chemotherapy (1-2 prior lines allowed)
       - Adjuvant/neoadjuvant chemotherapy completed < 12 months before
         recurrence counts as one line
IC-06: Adequate washout period:
       - Chemotherapy ≥ 21 days
       - Radiotherapy ≥ 14 days
       - Major surgery ≥ 28 days
IC-07: Adequate hematologic function:
       - Absolute neutrophil count (ANC) ≥ 1,500 cells/μL
       - Platelet count ≥ 100,000/μL
       - Hemoglobin ≥ 9.0 g/dL (transfusion allowed ≥ 14 days prior)
IC-08: Adequate hepatic function:
       - Total bilirubin ≤ 1.5 × ULN (≤ 3.0 × ULN if Gilbert's syndrome)
       - AST and ALT ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5.0 × ULN if liver metastases)
       - Alkaline phosphatase ≤ 2.5 × ULN (≤ 5.0 × ULN if bone/liver mets)
IC-09: Adequate renal function:
       - Serum creatinine ≤ 1.5 × ULN, OR
       - Calculated creatinine clearance ≥ 50 mL/min (Cockcroft-Gault)
IC-10: Coagulation: INR and aPTT ≤ 1.5 × ULN (unless on therapeutic
       anticoagulation)
IC-11: Thyroid function: TSH within normal range (supplementation allowed)
IC-12: Left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) ≥ 50% by echocardiogram
       or MUGA scan
IC-13: Negative pregnancy test for women of childbearing potential (WOCBP)
       within 7 days of randomization
IC-14: WOCBP and male patients with WOCBP partners must agree to use highly
       effective contraception during the study and for 120 days after the
       last dose
IC-15: Life expectancy ≥ 3 months as estimated by the investigator
IC-16: Ability to swallow oral medications (anlotinib is oral)
IC-17: Signed written informed consent prior to any study-specific procedures
IC-18: Willingness to undergo mandatory tumor biopsy at baseline and at
       disease progression (optional at response)

5. EXCLUSION CRITERIA (Must NOT meet ANY)

EC-01: Known EGFR sensitizing mutation (exon 19 deletion, L858R, G719X,
       L861Q, S768I), ALK rearrangement, ROS1 fusion, BRAF V600E
       mutation, MET exon 14 skipping, RET fusion, or NTRK fusion
       - Note: KRAS mutation, HER2 mutation, and PIK3CA mutation are
         allowed as no approved targeted therapy exists for these
         alterations in the second-line setting
EC-02: Mixed small cell and non-small cell histology
EC-03: Active central nervous system (CNS) metastases
       - Exception: Previously treated, asymptomatic CNS metastases
         that are stable for ≥ 4 weeks after completion of treatment
         (including steroids) are permitted
EC-04: Leptomeningeal carcinomatosis
EC-05: Active autoimmune disease requiring systemic immunosuppressive
       therapy within 2 years prior to randomization
       - Exceptions: vitiligo, alopecia, hypothyroidism on stable
         replacement therapy, type 1 diabetes on insulin, psoriasis
         not requiring systemic therapy
EC-06: History of interstitial lung disease (ILD), drug-induced ILD,
       radiation pneumonitis requiring steroids, or active pneumonitis
EC-07: Prior treatment with any anti-PD-1, anti-PD-L1, anti-PD-L2,
       anti-CTLA-4, anti-TIGIT, or anti-LAG3 antibody
       - Note: Prior anti-angiogenic TKI (except anlotinib) is permitted
EC-08: Uncontrolled hypertension despite optimal medical management
       (systolic > 150 mmHg or diastolic > 100 mmHg on 2 consecutive
       readings)
EC-09: Clinically significant bleeding within 3 months prior to
       randomization (hemoptysis > 2.5 mL, GI bleeding, tumor bleeding)
EC-10: Major blood vessel invasion by tumor (aorta, vena cava, pulmonary
       artery) as determined by CT imaging
EC-11: History of arterial or venous thromboembolism within 6 months
       (including TIA, CVA, DVT, PE)
EC-12: Proteinuria ≥ 2+ on dipstick or ≥ 1.0g/24-hour urine collection
EC-13: Active infection requiring systemic antibiotic, antiviral, or
       antifungal therapy within 14 days of randomization
EC-14: Known HIV infection
EC-15: Active Hepatitis B (HBsAg positive + HBV DNA > 2000 IU/mL) or
       Hepatitis C (anti-HCV positive + HCV RNA detectable)
EC-16: Concurrent malignancy requiring active treatment within 3 years
       (except adequately treated basal cell skin cancer, superficial
       bladder cancer, and carcinoma in situ of cervix)
EC-17: Pregnant or breastfeeding
EC-18: Any condition that, in the investigator's opinion, would interfere
       with study participation or interpretation of results
EC-19: Known hypersensitivity to toripalimab, anlotinib, docetaxel, or
       any of their excipients
EC-20: Receipt of live attenuated vaccine within 30 days of randomization

6. TREATMENT REGIMEN

6.1 Arm A — Experimental (N=60)

Toripalimab:
  - Dose: 240 mg
  - Route: Intravenous (IV) infusion over 60 minutes (first dose: 90 min)
  - Frequency: Every 3 weeks (Q3W) on Day 1 of each 21-day cycle
  - Pre-medication: Not routinely required. Acetaminophen 650 mg PO and
    diphenhydramine 25 mg IV/PO for Grade ≥ 2 infusion reactions

Anlotinib:
  - Dose: 12 mg
  - Route: Oral (PO), once daily
  - Schedule: 2 weeks on / 1 week off (Days 1-14 of each 21-day cycle)
  - Administration: With a glass of water, at approximately the same
    time each day, with or without food
  - Missed dose: If < 12 hours, take immediately. If ≥ 12 hours, skip
    and resume next scheduled dose

Dose Modifications:

Toripalimab dose reductions are NOT permitted. Management of immune-
related adverse events (irAEs) follows published guidelines (ASCO/NCCN).

Anlotinib dose reduction levels:
  - Level -1: 10 mg daily
  - Level -2: 8 mg daily
  - Discontinue if toxicity persists at 8 mg

Anlotinib should be withheld for:
  - Grade 3 hypertension not controlled within 48 hours
  - Grade ≥ 2 bleeding
  - Grade ≥ 3 proteinuria
  - Any arterial thromboembolic event
  - Wound dehiscence requiring medical intervention

6.2 Arm B — Control (N=60)

Docetaxel:
  - Dose: 75 mg/m²
  - Route: IV infusion over 60 minutes
  - Frequency: Every 3 weeks (Q3W) on Day 1
  - Pre-medication: Dexamethasone 8 mg PO BID for 3 days starting
    1 day before docetaxel administration

Dose Modifications:
  - Dose reduction 1: 60 mg/m²
  - Dose reduction 2: 50 mg/m²
  - Discontinue if toxicity persists at 50 mg/m²
  - G-CSF support allowed per ASCO guidelines

7. SAFETY MONITORING AND DLT DEFINITIONS

7.1 Adverse Event Assessment
- All AEs graded per CTCAE v5.0
- Serious adverse events (SAEs) reported within 24 hours
- AE attribution assessed by investigator (Unrelated / Unlikely /
  Possibly / Probably / Definitely related)

7.2 Dose-Limiting Toxicity (DLT) Evaluation Period
DLT observation window: Cycle 1 (first 21 days of treatment)

7.3 DLT Definitions

Hematologic DLTs:
  D-01: Grade 4 neutropenia lasting > 7 days despite G-CSF support
  D-02: Febrile neutropenia (ANC < 500/μL + temperature > 38.5°C)
  D-03: Grade 4 thrombocytopenia (< 25,000/μL)
  D-04: Grade 3 thrombocytopenia with clinically significant bleeding
        requiring platelet transfusion
  D-05: Grade 4 anemia (Hb < 6.5 g/dL)

Non-Hematologic DLTs:
  D-06: Grade ≥ 3 non-hematologic toxicity lasting > 48 hours despite
        optimal supportive care, EXCEPT:
        - Grade 3 nausea, vomiting, or diarrhea resolving to ≤ Grade 1
          within 72 hours with supportive care
        - Grade 3 fatigue resolving to ≤ Grade 1 within 7 days
        - Grade 3 electrolyte abnormalities corrected within 24 hours
        - Grade 3 infusion-related reaction resolving to ≤ Grade 1
          within 6 hours
        - Grade 3 hypertension controlled within 48 hours
  D-07: Any Grade 4 non-hematologic toxicity (except as above)
  D-08: Grade ≥ 2 pneumonitis (requires permanent discontinuation of
        toripalimab)
  D-09: Grade ≥ 3 colitis or hepatitis
  D-10: Grade ≥ 2 myocarditis, pericarditis, or myositis
  D-11: Any toxicity requiring permanent discontinuation of either
        study drug
  D-12: Treatment-related death

7.4 Blinded Independent Central Review (BICR)
All radiologic assessments (CT/MRI) will be reviewed by independent
central radiologists blinded to treatment assignment and clinical data.

7.5 Safety Monitoring Committee (SMC)
An independent SMC composed of 3 oncologists and 1 statistician will
review safety data at planned interim analyses.

7.6 Study Stopping Rules
The study will be temporarily suspended for SMC review if:

  S1: DLT rate in Arm A exceeds 30% among the first 20 evaluable
      patients (≥ 7 DLTs)
  S2: Any Grade 5 (fatal) adverse event assessed as "possibly",
      "probably", or "definitely" related to study treatment
  S3: ≥ 3 patients experience the same Grade 4 treatment-related AE
      of the same preferred term
  S4: ≥ 2 patients experience Grade ≥ 3 pneumonitis
  S5: ≥ 2 treatment-related deaths occur

The study may be terminated early by the sponsor or SMC if:
  - The safety profile is deemed unacceptable for the target population
  - The risk-benefit ratio becomes unfavorable based on emerging data

8. STUDY ASSESSMENTS AND SCHEDULE

8.1 Screening Period (Day -28 to Day -1)

Assessments:
  - Written informed consent
  - Complete medical and oncology history
  - Physical examination including height, weight, BSA
  - Vital signs (temperature, heart rate, blood pressure, respiratory
    rate, SpO2)
  - ECOG performance status
  - 12-lead ECG (QTcF interval calculation)
  - Echocardiogram or MUGA scan (LVEF)
  - CBC with differential and platelet count
  - Comprehensive chemistry panel (creatinine, BUN, glucose, albumin,
    calcium, magnesium, phosphate, total protein, LDH, uric acid)
  - Liver function tests (total/direct bilirubin, AST, ALT, ALP, GGT)
  - Coagulation panel (PT/INR, aPTT, fibrinogen)
  - Thyroid function (TSH, free T3, free T4)
  - Lipid panel (total cholesterol, HDL, LDL, triglycerides)
  - Urinalysis with microscopic examination
  - 24-hour urine protein collection (if dipstick ≥ 2+)
  - Pregnancy test (serum β-hCG) for WOCBP
  - Hepatitis B/C serology, HIV testing
  - Tumor biopsy (archival tissue accepted if collected ≤ 6 months
    prior; otherwise fresh biopsy required)
  - PD-L1 IHC testing (22C3 PharmDx assay)
  - Blood sample for biomarker analysis (ctDNA, TMB, multiplex IHC)
  - Contrast-enhanced CT scan of chest/abdomen/pelvis (CT-CAP)
  - Brain MRI with gadolinium (or contrast CT if MRI contraindicated)
  - Bone scan or FDG PET-CT (if clinically indicated)
  - Concomitant medications review
  - Adverse event assessment (baseline conditions)

8.2 Treatment Period (Cycles 1-35, each 21 days)

Day 1 of Every Cycle:
  - Physical examination, weight, vital signs
  - ECOG performance status
  - CBC with differential
  - Comprehensive chemistry panel (every cycle)
  - Liver function tests (every cycle)
  - Coagulation panel (Cycles 1-3, then every 3 cycles)
  - Thyroid function (every 2 cycles)
  - Lipid panel (every 2 cycles)
  - Urinalysis (every cycle)
  - Pregnancy test for WOCBP (every cycle)
  - Concomitant medications review
  - Adverse event assessment

Weekly During Cycle 1 (Days 8 and 15):
  - Vital signs, ECOG PS
  - CBC with differential
  - Chemistry panel
  - Adverse event assessment

Weekly During Cycle 2 (Days 8 and 15):
  - Vital signs, ECOG PS
  - CBC with differential
  - Adverse event assessment

Cycles 3+:
  - Day 1 assessments only (as above)

Tumor Assessments:
  - CT-CAP every 6 weeks (± 7 days) for the first 6 months
  - CT-CAP every 9 weeks (± 7 days) thereafter
  - Brain MRI every 12 weeks (or more frequently if brain metastases
    present at baseline)
  - Bone scan or PET-CT every 6 months if bone metastases at baseline

Response Assessment:
  - RECIST v1.1 criteria for all patients
  - Confirmation of CR or PR required with repeat imaging ≥ 4 weeks
    after initial documentation of response
  - Patients with SD must have SD documented at least 6 weeks after
    randomization

Quality of Life:
  - EORTC QLQ-C30 and QLQ-LC13 questionnaires at baseline
  - Every 6 weeks during treatment
  - At end of treatment visit

Biomarker Sampling:
  - Blood for ctDNA analysis: baseline, Day 1 of Cycles 2, 3, 5, and
    every 3 cycles thereafter, and at disease progression
  - Blood for multiplex IHC/biomarker panel: baseline, Cycle 3 Day 1,
    and at disease progression
  - Optional on-treatment tumor biopsy: Cycle 3 Day 1 (window: Day 15-21)

8.3 End of Treatment (EOT) Visit (within 30 days of last dose)

  - Physical examination, weight, vital signs
  - ECOG performance status
  - CBC with differential and chemistry panel
  - Liver function tests, coagulation
  - 12-lead ECG, echocardiogram
  - Thyroid function, lipid panel
  - Urinalysis
  - Pregnancy test (WOCBP)
  - CT-CAP (unless performed within previous 4 weeks)
  - Brain MRI (if brain metastases at baseline, unless within 4 weeks)
  - Concomitant medications review
  - Adverse event assessment (resolution documentation)
  - Quality of life questionnaires
  - Blood for biomarker analysis

8.4 Safety Follow-up Visit (30 days ± 7 days after last dose)

  - Adverse event assessment (focus on AE resolution)
  - Concomitant medications
  - Assessment of ongoing irAEs and management plan

8.5 Long-Term Follow-up (every 12 weeks ± 14 days after EOT)

  - Survival status (alive/dead, date and cause of death if applicable)
  - Subsequent anti-cancer therapy received
  - Survival follow-up continues for 24 months from randomization
    or until death, withdrawal of consent, or study closure

9. STATISTICAL ANALYSIS PLAN

9.1 Sample Size Determination

  - Primary endpoint: PFS (progression-free survival)
  - Hypothesis: Superiority of Arm A over Arm B
  - Median PFS assumption: Arm A 6.5 months vs Arm B 4.0 months
  - Hazard ratio: 0.615
  - Accrual period: 18 months
  - Follow-up period: 12 months after last patient in
  - One-sided α = 0.025 (adjusted for one interim analysis)
  - Power = 80%
  - Total events required: 98 PFS events
  - Total sample size: 120 patients (60 per arm), accounting for 10%
    dropout rate

9.2 Interim Analysis

  - One planned interim analysis for futility at 50% of events (49 PFS
    events)
  - O'Brien-Fleming spending function for alpha
  - Interim analysis will be performed by an independent statistician
  - SMC will review unblinded results and make recommendations
  - The primary analysis will be conducted when 98 PFS events have been
    observed

9.3 Primary Endpoint Analysis

  - PFS defined as time from randomization to first documented disease
    progression per RECIST v1.1 (BICR) or death from any cause
  - Patients alive and progression-free at analysis cutoff will be
    censored at last tumor assessment date
  - Primary analysis: stratified log-rank test with stratification
    factors (PD-L1 TPS, histology, prior treatment lines)
  - Hazard ratio and 95% CI estimated using stratified Cox proportional
    hazards model
  - Median PFS and 95% CI estimated by Kaplan-Meier method
  - Sensitivity analysis: PFS by investigator assessment

9.4 Secondary Endpoint Analyses (Hierarchical Testing)

  1. OS: stratified log-rank test (tested if PFS is significant)
  2. ORR: Cochran-Mantel-Haenszel test stratified by randomization
     factors; odds ratio and 95% CI reported
  3. DCR: same method as ORR
  4. Safety: descriptive statistics, CTCAE v5.0 grade, system organ
     class, preferred term
  5. QoL: mixed-effects model for repeated measures (MMRM) for EORTC
     QLQ-C30 global health status score

9.5 Subgroup Analyses (Exploratory)

  - PD-L1 TPS subgroups: < 1%, 1-49%, ≥ 50%
  - Histology: squamous vs non-squamous
  - Prior treatment lines: 1 vs 2
  - TMB: high (≥ 10 mut/Mb) vs low (< 10 mut/Mb)
  - bTMB: high vs low (cutoff to be determined from Phase Ib data)
  - Presence of liver metastases
  - Age: < 65 vs ≥ 65 years
  - Sex: male vs female
  - Smoking status: never vs former/current

9.6 Sample Size Re-estimation

  - Adaptive sample size re-estimation based on pooled event rate at
    interim analysis (blinded)
  - Maximum increase of 40 patients (20 per arm)
  - Conducted by independent statistician; details not disclosed to
    investigators or sponsor to maintain blinding
